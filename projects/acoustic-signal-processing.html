Acoustic Signal Processing & Feature Extraction

Problem

High-rate acoustic recordings contain meaningful structure but are dominated by noise, nonstationarity, and artifacts. The challenge was to extract stable, interpretable features suitable for downstream modeling.

Approach

  • Bandpass filtering and denoising of raw acoustic signals

  • Amplitude normalization and alignment across trials
  • Dimensionality reduction using PCA to capture dominant structure
  • Validation via reconstruction error and predictive modeling

Results

The resulting feature representations were significantly more stable than raw signals and enabled reliable prediction of neural firing rates using regression and tree-based models.

PCA revealed low-dimensional structure that aligned with meaningful acoustic events, while removing redundant or noisy dimensions.

Engineering Takeaways

This project reinforced the importance of validating signal processing assumptions before applying machine learning. Careful preprocessing and feature design dramatically improved model stability and interpretability.
